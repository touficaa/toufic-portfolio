The athlete

From overweight and bullied at thirteen, to a competitive endurance athlete at nineteen — built one race at a time, without shortcuts.

I am Toufic Abou Ali, a Lebanese endurance athlete based in Montpellier, France. I compete in road racing and triathlon while building a career and a company in parallel. My athletic identity is built on one principle: showing up regardless of conditions, pressure, or pain.

I started training at thirteen after deciding I would not stay the way I was. I ran my first half marathon at sixteen and won. I ran my first marathon at eighteen and stood on the podium. At nineteen I will line up for my first Half Ironman in Warsaw — seven days before turning twenty.
